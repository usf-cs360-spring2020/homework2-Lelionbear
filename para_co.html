Analysis

By designing a parallel coordinate visualization I am able to visualize the data gathered by the "Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility". The parameters I selected to use were the par_q1 (fraction of parents in 1st income quintile, lowest quintile), type (public, private non-profit, for profit), k_q5 (fraction of kids in 5th income quintile, highest quintile), and female (fraction female among kids). For additional information I used the types of school years (4-year, 2-year, less than two years) for the color scale. By observing the visualization, we can see all fractional parents in the poorest quintile and see what fraction of them were females who managed to reach the 5th quintile. We can also compare the outcomes from the type of school duration each student attended, 4, 2, or less than 2 as a factor for their mobility shift. From the visualization we can prove that the findings of the "Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility" provide some evidence of female quintile shift to be more successful in a less than two year enrollment. This corelation is incomplete as more data would need to be collected to fully provide a reasonable conclusion.
